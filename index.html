I'm Agathasta. 

This is how I discovered the world of code.

In spring 2020, with time in my hands, I decided to try and give my WordPress business webpage a complete overhaul, both in design and content. It was my first contact ever with code. I got curious, and by summer 2020 I was doing all the free "teach yourself how to code" courses I could find. All of them! A month later, I had managed to get some structure, and settled mostly on two open source free resources: freeCodeCamp.org and The Odin Project, with sporadic jumps to and tons of help from MDN web docs and w3schools. I was hooked.

In fall I went back-end. JavaScript showed me the world of algorithms, and I loved them... until I hit a brick wall. I had to slow down, and start learning some basic concepts. I shifted towards the courses by The Odin Project and other Vikings around, and thus I met Ruby. Ruby is still being a different dimension, and I'm still quite struggling with the abstraction, but somehow I'm getting on. I notice I'm developing a quite interesting stubborn streak.

The web is being again an incredible help. I'm doing daily challenges at edabit and following the Ruby track at Exercism. The community at StackOverflow is not less valued for being obvious, as are Jesus Castello's RubyGuides, but there is a ton of others (TODO: make a list!!).
